This application takes in (currently) three parameters: the Key, if it is Major or Minor, and the Scale Degree. There are 12 Keys in western music. One for every note of an octave (C, C#, D, etc.). Within the major and minor scales of western music there are seven notes. These can be though of as Scale Degrees. These Scale Degrees can be used to construct chords within the key, and all will fit together nicely when played as part of a chord progression. For example: the 1st chord of a scale can be constructed by combining the 1st, 3rd and 5th degrees of the scale together to form a chord. In the Key of A Major, that will be the chord A Major.

For more information on music theory this link will provide valuable context!

While the note Cb/B# may seem nonsensicle to many it is indeed used, as is Fb/E#, for certain key signatures. In my application it will only show up for the key of F#/Gb. This is partly due to the limitations of the VexFlow.js library.
